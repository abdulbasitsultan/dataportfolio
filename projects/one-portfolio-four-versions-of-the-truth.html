Analytics Engineering · BI Development · Stakeholder Reporting

One Portfolio. Four Versions of the Truth.

Four teams were working from disconnected reporting sources across cost, cashflow, progress and schedule reporting. I built a unified Power BI reporting layer that standardised project identities, separated actuals from forecasts and gave stakeholders one agreed view for reviews.

The Problem

Nobody was wrong. Nobody agreed.

Cost, cashflow, progress and schedule data lived in separate files with different naming, codes and monthly structures. Meetings often started with teams reconciling numbers before they could talk about decisions.

What I Built

A reporting layer before dashboard polish.

I standardised project identities, separated actuals from forecasts using the reporting date, reshaped monthly spreadsheet data into long format and wrote DAX measures that stayed reliable across project, region, campus, date and delivery stage filters.

Commercial Exposure

€977M needed more than a headline number.

The waterfall made contingency movement readable. It showed the path from approved contingency through spend, change requests, risk weighted value and remaining balance. The commercial view also made €312M committed, €665M uncommitted and €43M invoiced visible in one place.

Cashflow Timing

The reporting date separated facts from forecasts.

The cashflow curve used a marker to show which values were confirmed actuals and which were future projections. That stopped forecast values from being treated as confirmed spend.

Capacity Concentration

Frankfurt carried 67 percent of delivery capacity.

The capacity view showed 12 active projects and 708MW total capacity. Frankfurt carried 476MW, West Africa carried 148MW and Central East carried 84MW, which made concentration risk visible.

Schedule Performance

Hours logged were not the same as progress.

The schedule view connected plan versus actual hours, SPI trend and task completion. The SPI dropped to 24 percent before recovering to 86 percent, while the variance reached negative 7,017 hours.

What It Changed

The reporting layer removed repeated reconciliation from decision making.

Commercial teams could track budget status. Finance could validate cashflow month by month. Leadership could monitor capacity and readiness milestones. Project managers could see schedule variance before the review started.
